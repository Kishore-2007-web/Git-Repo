CHARLIE CHAPPLEN

Charlie Chaplin was an English comic actor, filmmaker, and composer who rose to fame in the silent film era.
He is best known for his character "The Tramp," a comedic persona that became iconic in the world of cinema.
         Chaplin's films often combined humor with social commentary, addressing issues such as poverty, class struggle, and the human condition. 
Some of his most famous works include "The Kid," "City Lights," "Modern Times," and "The Great Dictator."
          Chaplin's influence on the film industry is profound, and he remains one of the most celebrated figures in cinematic history.
